When the Gods Bathe

June 12, 2025

Wishing you all an auspicious Debsnana Purnima

Today,
the gods bathe not to cleanse,
but to remind us—
even the eternal touches earth,
even the infinite allows itself
to be held in ritual.

Jagannath,
Balabhadra,
Subhadra—
not idols today,
but living presence.
They are brought down
from the golden seat,
step by step,
in rhythm with conches,
in the music of surrender.

At the Snana Mandap,
the waters wait—
108 pots,
filled not just with water,
but with longing,
with the weight of generations
who believe
without question.

The first drop falls—
and the heavens pause.
It is not just a bath,
it is a covenant.
Between divinity and devotion.
Between silence and song.

This is no spectacle.
It is a lesson—
that the gods walk with us,
bathe with us,
suffer with us,
and rise again,
just as we must.
